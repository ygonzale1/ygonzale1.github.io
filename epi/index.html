Environmental Epidemiological Analysis

Pollution • Incidence • Correlations • Peaks • Trends • Delayed Impact

📊 How this tool works

🎯 Purpose

Analyze the relationship between air pollution (PM₂.₅, NO₂, O₃, etc.) and epidemiological incidence rates. Explore delayed effects (lag), detect extreme incidence peaks, and visualize seasonal trends.

📁 Data requirements

  • date (YYYY-MM-DD) and incidencia_1000 are mandatory.
  • Optional: pm2.5, pm10, no2, nox,no, o3, so2.
  • CSV format with header, commas or semicolons supported.

🚀 How to use

  1. Select a preloaded city dataset or upload your own CSV using "Browse".
  2. Adjust time filters to focus on specific periods.
  3. Explore lagged correlations (0–7 days) for delayed impact.
  4. WHO Relative Risk: Automatically calculated when a pollutant with a WHO threshold is selected. Includes Attributable Risk.
  5. Peaks table shows statistically unusual incidence days (mean + 2*SD).
  6. Monthly bar chart reveals seasonal patterns.

📈 Correlation & p-values

Pearson's r + significance. p < 0.05 indicates association.
⚠️ Asterisks (*) indicate statistical significance (p < 0.05 = *, p < 0.01 = **, p < 0.001 = ***). They do NOT measure the strength of association.

⚠️ Peak detection

Threshold = mean + 2×standard deviation.

🩺 Epidemiological Risk

Computes Relative Risk (RR) and Attributable Fraction (AR%) with 95% CI and p-values.

📡 Data sources & acknowledgments

Respiratory infection data obtained from SIVIC server (Generalitat de Catalunya).
Air pollution data obtained from XPVCA server (Generalitat de Catalunya).
Meteorological data obtained from Meteocat (Generalitat de Catalunya).
